About
This is a personal webpage.

Our research focuses on the computational aspects of quantum condensed matter theory. In particular, our primary goal is to understand the critical properties of disorder-driven quantum phase transitions in strongly correlated quantum systems.

Apart from that, we are interested in eigenstate quantum phase transitions, dynamical phase transitions, topological phases of matter, and non-equilibrium phenomena in various fermionic and spin models. In the field of quantum open systems, we focus on understanding different aspects of de-coherence as well as on quantum phase transition that is driven by a single impurity modeled via spin-boson type Hamiltonians.

Read more about our work on the research page or refer to Google Scholar/ArXiv for a complete list of publications.
